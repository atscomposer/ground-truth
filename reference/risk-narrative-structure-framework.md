# Reference: Risk Narrative Structure Framework

Use this framework to evaluate structure (Rule 7) — not to write structure for the writer. When a narrative's structure buries the risk, you can point to this framework by name to explain what's missing, but the writer builds the actual structure.

## The Risk-Exposure-Ask (R-E-A) Structure

A well-structured identity risk narrative answers three questions, roughly in this order:

1. **Risk** — What is the finding or condition? Stated in one or two sentences, no preamble, no process narrative first.
2. **Exposure** — What does this actually put in jeopardy, and how badly or how likely? This is where the "so what" (Rule 2) lives — tied to a named consequence category:
   - Financial exposure (cost of breach, fines, remediation spend)
   - Operational disruption (systems down, processes blocked)
   - Regulatory/legal action (audit finding, violation, litigation exposure)
   - Reputational event (customer trust, media, partner relationships)
   - Breach pathway (this specific gap is how an attacker would get in or move laterally)
3. **Ask** — What does the reader need to do: approve, decide, fund, accept the risk formally, or simply retain awareness ahead of a known date?

## Diagnosing Structural Failures Against R-E-A

- **Buried risk**: the Risk statement appears after unrelated status/process content. (See Rule 7.)
- **Missing exposure**: Risk is stated, but no Exposure follows — reader knows *what* but not *why it matters*. (See Rule 2.)
- **Missing ask**: Risk and Exposure are both present, but the narrative doesn't tell the reader what to do with the information. (See Rule 6.)
- **Inverted structure**: some narratives lead with the Ask ("we need budget for X") before establishing Risk or Exposure — this can work for a sponsor who already has context, but fails for a board or committee encountering the issue cold. Flag if the audience doesn't already have the context assumed.

## A Note on BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front)

R-E-A is a variant of BLUF discipline applied specifically to risk communication. The "bottom line" for a risk narrative is never just the headline metric — it's the Risk + Exposure combination. A BLUF that states a metric without exposure is still burying the actual bottom line.

## Using This Framework in a Critique

Reference the framework by name when useful ("this reads as Risk without Exposure — see the R-E-A framework"), but the fix itself — how to restructure, what exposure to name — stays the writer's job per Rule 0.
